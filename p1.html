• Anxiety Disorders

    Conditions characterized by excessive worry, fear, and nervousness that can significantly impact daily life. This category includes various specific disorders like generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and phobias.

  • Depression

    A mood disorder causing persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, loss of interest or pleasure in activities, affecting daily life functioning. Major depressive disorder is one of the most common forms.

  • Bipolar Disorder

    A mood disorder characterized by extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression). It involves shifts in energy, activity levels, and the ability to carry out day-to-day tasks.

  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

    An anxiety disorder that can develop after exposure to a traumatic event. Symptoms may include flashbacks, nightmares, severe anxiety, and uncontrollable thoughts about the event.

  • Schizophrenia

    A chronic and severe mental disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. It may cause hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking, and impairments in social and occupational functioning.

  • Eating Disorders

    Conditions characterized by unhealthy attitudes and behaviors toward food and weight. Examples include anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder.

  • Disruptive Behaviour and Dissocial Disorders

    This category includes conditions involving persistent patterns of behavior that significantly violate social norms and the rights of others. Conduct disorder and oppositional defiant disorder are examples.

  • Neurodevelopmental Disorders

    Conditions typically diagnosed in childhood, characterized by developmental deficits affecting personal, social, academic, and occupational functioning. Examples include attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), autism spectrum disorder, and specific learning disorders.
